# Woahh Shop — Competitive Research & Feature Analysis

**Research Date:** May 2026
**Scope:** Global grocery & retail apps competitive analysis
**Target:** Small independent grocers, specialty food, and retail businesses

---

## Executive Summary

1. Small businesses CAN compete online but need critical infrastructure in place
2. Fulfillment flexibility (pickup, local delivery, scheduled slots) is table-stakes, not a differentiator
3. Large chains' advantages: logistics scale, brand recognition, financial capacity
4. Small business advantages: personalisation, community, quality narrative, faster adaptation
5. Woahh's unique opportunity: cross-restaurant/retail features, local-first positioning, software + fulfillment for independent merchants

## Part 2 — Feature Deep-Dive Analysis

### Fulfillment Models

#### Essential Modes for Woahh Shop MVP

| Mode | Use Case | Hardware Needed | Complexity |
|---|---|---|---|
| Click & Collect (BOPIS) | Core for all shops | Optional: refrigerated lockers | Medium |
| Same-day local delivery | Competitive necessity | Vehicle; optional route optimisation software | High |
| Scheduled delivery | Planned shopping | Calendar integration, route planning | High |
| Parcel shipping | Non-perishables, national | Carrier API integration | Low |

**Key insight:** Small businesses typically CAN'T compete on ultra-fast (<1 hour) without logistics infrastructure. Position Woahh as "reliable scheduled delivery for locals" rather than speed-based competition.

#### Click & Collect Lockers
Companies like Click n Collect and Penguin Lockers offer refrigerated locker systems:
- Ambient, chilled, and frozen compartments
- 24/7 access via PIN/QR code
- Real-time sync with order management systems
- Reduces delivery reattempts and carbon footprint
- **Estimated cost (AU):** $5,000–$20,000 per unit

### Product Catalog & Special Categories

#### Weight-Based Pricing (Produce, Deli, Bakery, Butcher)

Critical for specialty grocers. Solutions in Shopify ecosystem:
- **Measura:** sell by weight, length, area, volume; auto-adjusts inventory
- **Filljoy:** weight-based POS, handles tare weights
- **Alumiya:** grocery, spice, bulk, butcher specialist
- **Unitpricer:** volume discounts

**How it works:**
- Customer selects item, inputs weight/measurement
- System calculates price automatically
- Inventory decrements fractionally
- Tare weight handling for containers (deli)

**Required for:** butchers, fishmongers, produce, deli, bakery (bulk bread/pastry)

#### Deli & Bakery Made-to-Order

Major chains (Safeway, Albertsons, Whole Foods) offer Order Ahead for deli/meat/seafood/bakery. Key requirements:
- Prep time setting per item
- Customisation options (e.g. "less salt," "extra thick cut")
- Ingredient-level inventory
- **Woahh advantage:** reuse restaurant KDS for made-to-order fulfillment flow

#### Nutrition & Allergen Compliance

- Mandatory allergen info available only ~35% of the time across major US retailers (compliance gap)
- **Solutions:** Sifter (12+ allergens, FALCPA compliant), FatSecret API (2.3M+ foods, 58 countries), Open Food Facts (open-source, community-driven)
- **Woahh approach:** merchant-verified allergen tags for fresh goods + third-party database for packaged goods

### Inventory Management & Demand Forecasting

#### AI Demand Forecasting (Becoming mainstream)

**What it does:**
- ML models analyse: past sales, seasonality, promotions, weather, holidays, events
- Predict future demand with 90–95% accuracy (vs. legacy 85% baseline)
- Auto-generate purchase orders with suggested quantities

**Business impact:**
- Reduce inventory costs 20–35%
- Prevent 65% of stockouts
- Reduce manual ordering time by 76%
- Critical for fresh goods: produce, meat, dairy (spoilage control)

**Providers:** Symphony AI, OrderGrid, Leafio, Invent.AI

**For Woahh:** Integrate lightweight demand forecasting API in Phase 2–3. Small shops benefit enormously — fresher products, less waste, better margins.

#### Multi-Location Inventory
- Shopify POS Pro and Lightspeed both support centralised view, inter-location transfers, location-level reporting
- Relevant for merchants with 2–5 locations (Phase 2 feature for Woahh Shop)

### Staff-Facing Features

#### Order Picking & Packing

**Standard workflow:**
1. Pick list sent to staff (mobile or printed)
2. Staff navigate store with handheld barcode scanner
3. Scan item barcode + storage location → validates pick
4. System guides to next item (prevents error, speeds picking)

**Technology:** Orderpicking App, PULPO WMS, Veeqo
**Hardware:** Honeywell, Zebra handheld scanners (~$200–500 AUD)

**For Woahh MVP:** printable pick list. Phase 2: barcode scan validation.

#### Route Optimisation

**Providers:**
- **Onfleet:** AI-powered; cuts fuel 45%, increases driver capacity 30–50%, 95–98% on-time delivery
- **Routific:** Built for recurring deliveries (meal kits, pharmacy, grocery)
- **EasyRoutes, Upper, Locus:** grocery-specific

**Key features:** order clustering, route optimisation, driver availability + shop capacity constraints, delivery time slot commitments, real-time tracking, traffic-aware routing

**For Woahh:** integrate Routific or Onfleet API in Phase 2. MVP: manual dispatch + map view.

#### Customer Communication During Fulfillment

Current best practice:
- Proactive notification of delays/substitutions
- Allow customer approval before substituting
- Real-time chat with picker (optional)
- Delivery tracking if using courier

**For Woahh:** SMS/email notifications (already built in restaurant side). Web order status page. Real-time tracking in Phase 2.

| Ultra-fast delivery (15 min–1 hr) | Requires micro-fulfillment centres, millions in capex | Don't compete; position as "reliable, local" |

| Proprietary ML demand forecasting | Requires years of data + engineering teams | Integrate 3rd-party APIs (OrderGrid, Leafio) |
| Brand trust at scale | Woolworths/Coles are household names | Amplify local merchant narrative through Woahh |
| 40K–50K SKU catalogs | Space + logistics constraints | Curate 500–3K high-quality SKUs; quality > quantity |

### What Small Shops Do BETTER (That Woahh Can Amplify)

**1. Human-level personalisation**
- Know customers by name, preferences, family situation
- Hand-pick produce/meat for regulars
- Woahh opportunity: "Shopkeeper's note" feature — "Sarah picked this Manuka honey for you"

**2. Product quality & freshness**
- Smaller turnover = fresher inventory
- Direct relationships with producers
- Woahh opportunity: rich product storytelling — photo of producer, origin story, tasting notes

**3. Flexibility & customisation**
- "Can you de-bone this chicken?" done in 5 minutes
- Custom sausages, specialty cuts, bread for dietary needs
- Woahh opportunity: made-to-order workflow with KDS integration (reuse restaurant side)

**4. Community connection**
- Shop is a gathering place, not a warehouse
- Owner knows customers' families and celebrations
- Woahh opportunity: community features — event calendar, producer Q&A, local reviews

**5. Faster adaptation**
- One owner can decide "more gluten-free" in a week
- Large chains: committee decisions, 3-month lead times
- Woahh opportunity: merchant empowerment — easy inventory changes, quick promotions, test new suppliers

## Part 5 — Gaps Nobody Is Filling (Woahh's Edge)

### 1. Online + Community Bridging
Apps are transactional (browse, buy, pickup). No storytelling, no "meet the producer," no local narrative.
**Opportunity:** Social/community layer — local reviews, producer Q&A, event calendar, neighbourhood feed

### 2. Cross-Category Bundling (Restaurant + Shop)
Grocery apps don't integrate with café/deli/hot prepared food. Customers buy groceries OR ready-made meals.
**Woahh's unique advantage:** "Order a fresh salad AND the ingredients to make pasta at home" — bundle restaurant + shop orders. This is exclusively available to Woahh because we have both sides.

### 3. Hyper-Local Supply Chain Transparency
No app shows: "What's in stock TODAY because it just arrived from the farm this morning."
**Opportunity:** Real-time producer connections — "Fresh tomatoes from Sunny Hills Farm, arrived 2 hours ago"

### 4. Subscriptions for Specialty Shops
Recurring orders exist for Safeway/Woolworths. Missing entirely for butchers, bakers, specialty grocers.
**Opportunity:** "Weekly grass-fed beef box from your local butcher, $80, recurring every Friday"

### 5. Staff Empowerment for Customisation
POS tracks inventory but doesn't enable staff to handle custom requests in digital orders.
**Opportunity:** Flexible order management — staff can add notes, adjust prices, add custom items, communicate directly with customer

### 6. Unified Fulfillment Across Modes
Click & Collect, delivery, and in-store shopping are separate UX flows in all existing platforms.
**Opportunity (reuse restaurant fulfillment):** "Add to cart — decide pickup/delivery later" — seamless mode switching at checkout, same as restaurant fulfillment type selection

## Part 8 — Strategic Recommendations

### Don't Try to Out-DoorDash
- Skip 15-minute delivery (requires heavy logistics capex)
- Focus on 2–4 hour reliable delivery
- Position: "Your local shop — online, finally"

### Start With Existing Woahh Restaurant Merchants
- They know the platform, trust it, use it daily
- Natural cross-sell: butcher who also sells ready-made pies; bakery that also sells flour
- Built-in customer base already ordering on Woahh
- Higher LTV and retention than cold-start merchants

### Hardware Strategy for Shop
- Offer optional hardware bundle (label scale + printer + tablet) at cost + margin
- Partner with CAS Scales Australia and CompuBox for supply
- All-in Marketplace Shop Kit: tablet + label scale + receipt printer — ~$4,500 COGS, ~$6,500 startup fee, ~$2,000 margin (44%)
- Existing Solo/Marketplace hardware kits extend with a label scale add-on

### Loyalty: Don't Compete on Points Scale
- Don't try to build Flybuys or Everyday Rewards
- Offer simple: per-shop points ("earn $5 every $100") or cross-merchant Woahh subscription
- **Unique play:** "Follow your favourite butcher" — community-based loyalty vs. transactional points
- Let Woahh's existing CRM and loyalty infrastructure handle the engine

### AI: Integrate, Don't Build
- Demand forecasting: integrate OrderGrid or Leafio API (Phase 2)
- Substitution recommendations: lightweight rules engine first, ML later
- Recipe discovery: leverage ChatGPT/Claude API (already in feature backlog)
- Recommendations: start with simple "frequently bought together" before ML models
