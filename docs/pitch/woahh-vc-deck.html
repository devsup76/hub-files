Why now

The window just opened.

  • AI killed the hard setup. A menu reads from a photo into a live catalog and AI now drafts the recipes — the two setup blockers (catalog + inventory) are gone.
  • Margin pressure is acute. Food-cost inflation and volatile produce prices are crushing thin margins — owners urgently need to know their real plate costs.
  • The stack is unbundleable. Owners are tired of paying four vendors that don't talk; appetite for one smart system is real.
  • Commodity infra + cause-aligned commerce. A kitchen screen runs on a $40 stick; giving is now a growth lever, not a cost.

This product wasn't buildable — or sellable — three years ago.

The differentiator

Inventory that runs — and thinks — for you.

1 · Runs itself · built

AI recipes + auto-deplete

AI drafts every recipe from your menu. Stock drops automatically as orders complete; sell-outs auto-pull the dish from your storefront. No clipboards.

2 · Learns · roadmap

Predicts your stock

A few stock-takes teach it how much each dish really uses — so it predicts your stock and cuts manual counting ~75%. No incumbent closes this loop.

3 · Flagship · roadmap

Prices every plate

Knows the live cost & margin of every dish as ingredient prices swing — and tells you when to run a sale and when not to.

MarketMan and MarginEdge tell you a price went up. Woahh tells you what to cook, what to push, and what to put on special — and donates half of what it earns. A profit copilot, not a cost report.
