## You're overpaying for a patchwork

Most independent restaurants stitch together a stack like this:

- **Square (~$165/mo)** for POS
- **Uber Eats / DoorDash (25–30% commission)** for online orders
- **A separate website builder** for your menu
- **A separate mailing tool** for customer email

On a $50k/month restaurant, that's **$5,000–$6,000 a month** — and after all of it, you *still don't own a single customer email address.* The delivery apps own your customers, your reviews, and your relationship. You're renting access to your own diners.

## Three things the big platforms can't offer

### 1. An operating system **and** its own demand channel
Square and Toast give you tools but no marketplace. DoorDash and Uber own the marketplace but take 25–30% and keep your customer. woahh gives you the full back-of-house stack **and** a discovery marketplace where you own the diner — and a single customer account that follows that diner across every woahh restaurant.

### 2. Giving back is built into the rails — and it's *your* marketing
**We commit 50% of every subscription to charity** — concrete, transparent, and it applies to everyone from day one. You can dial your own give-back higher anytime with a simple slider. Every dollar is written to a public impact ledger with a cause-by-cause breakdown and a merchant leaderboard, and your storefront carries an **Impact Partner badge**. Your customers see exactly what their meal helped fund — so giving becomes a reason people choose you. *No major delivery app or POS donates from sales at all.*

### 3. AI that does the tedious work
- **Menu import from a photo or PDF** — reads your existing menu into an editable table of categories, items and prices, ready to publish in one click.
- **Campaign copywriter** — describe your goal and tone; it drafts your SMS and email.
- **Decline-reason suggestions** — friendly, customer-ready wording when you have to turn an order away.
