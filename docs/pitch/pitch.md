## The World Has Changed. Small Businesses Haven't Caught Up.

Every major restaurant chain and retailer in the world operates a full technology stack.
Dominos has its own app. Shake Shack runs its own loyalty program. McDonald's owns its customer data, sends push notifications, and re-engages lapsed customers with targeted offers.

Small business owners — the café on your corner, the family restaurant, the independent boutique — have none of this.

They're fighting billion-dollar brands with a Square terminal and a Facebook page.

The technology gap isn't just unfair. It's compounding. While big brands build deeper customer relationships every year, small businesses lose customers to whichever app happens to have the biggest marketing budget that week. They don't own a single customer email address. They don't know who visited twice and stopped. They can't send a birthday reward.

Meanwhile, the platforms they depend on are extracting value at every step.

A merchant processing $20,000/month through Woahh donates ~$30 to charity automatically — and their storefront displays an Impact Partner badge that tells that story to every customer.

We donate approximately 20% of total revenue to charity. No competitor is within an order of magnitude of this commitment.

## Why This Works

**1. No competitor does both axes.**
Square and Toast give you operational tools but no marketplace discovery.
Uber Eats and DoorDash give you discovery but take 30% and you never own the customer.
Woahh does both — your own full-stack presence AND zero-commission marketplace listing — under one subscription.

**2. The mission drives acquisition.**
Word of mouth is the primary customer acquisition channel for small businesses. A café owner who tells three other café owners "this platform donates to charity and costs less than my phone bill" is worth more than any Google ad. The giving model is not a cost centre — it is a growth engine.

**3. The numbers work at every scale.**
At 100 merchants: profitable from day one.
At 1,000 merchants: $149,000/month MRR, ~$298,000/year going to charity.
At 5,000 merchants: $745,000/month MRR, ~$1.49M/year in charitable impact.

**4. Churn is structurally low.**
A merchant who has built their customer list in Woahh's CRM, run loyalty campaigns, and integrated their delivery flow does not leave easily. The switching cost is their customer data, their loyalty history, and their brand presence — all of which live inside Woahh.

## The /eat Marketplace Changes Everything

The `/eat` marketplace is Woahh's second product, and it shifts the competitive landscape entirely.

Today, a customer who wants to find a new local restaurant opens Uber Eats. The restaurant pays 25–30% to be discovered. Uber keeps the customer relationship.

The `/eat` marketplace lists every Marketplace and Growth tier merchant at zero commission. Customers discover local businesses. Merchants keep 100% of revenue. The customer relationship — the email, the order history, the loyalty points — belongs to the merchant.

This is Shopify's argument applied to food and retail: you should own your customers, not rent them from a platform.

The marketplace also introduces network effects. More merchants make the directory more useful for customers. More customers make the listing more valuable for merchants. A flywheel that direct competitors can't easily replicate.
