Notwithstanding any other provision of this Agreement, no issuance of Shares, options, convertible notes, or any other equity or equity-equivalent instruments shall be made, and no conversion, exercise, or capitalisation event shall occur, if the effect of such event would reduce the voting rights of the CEO Shareholder (calculated on a fully diluted basis) below twenty-five percent (25%) of the total voting rights of the Company (the **"CEO Voting Floor"**), unless the CEO Shareholder has given their prior individual written consent to that specific issuance or event.

For the avoidance of doubt:

(a) the CEO Voting Floor applies regardless of any unanimous Board or Shareholder resolution approving such issuance;

(b) the CEO Voting Floor survives any future amendment to this Agreement unless the CEO Shareholder individually and expressly agrees in writing to its removal; and

(c) at any future investment round where the CEO Voting Floor would otherwise be breached, the Company and the CEO Shareholder agree to use best endeavours to implement a dual-class share structure (Class A ordinary shares for investors carrying one vote per share; Class B shares held by the Founders carrying ten votes per share) so as to preserve the CEO Shareholder's effective voting control. The Shareholders agree to cooperate in good faith to document such restructure prior to, or simultaneously with, the relevant investment round.

## 3. VESTING

### 3.1 Vesting Schedule

Each Shareholder's Shares are subject to the following vesting schedule (the **"Vesting Schedule"**):

(a) **Cliff:** No Shares vest during the first twelve (12) months following the Vesting Commencement Date (the **"Cliff Period"**).

(b) **Initial Vest:** On the first anniversary of the Vesting Commencement Date, twenty-five percent (25%) of each Shareholder's total Shares vest immediately.

(c) **Monthly Vesting:** Following the Cliff Period, the remaining seventy-five percent (75%) of each Shareholder's total Shares vest in equal monthly instalments over the following thirty-six (36) months, such that all Shares are fully vested at the end of the four (4) year Vesting Period.

### 3.2 Acceleration on Exit

Upon an Exit Event, all unvested Shares held by each Shareholder who remains actively involved in the Business at the time of the Exit Event shall vest immediately and in full (single-trigger acceleration).

### 3.3 Unvested Shares — Good Leaver

If a Shareholder becomes a Good Leaver, their unvested Shares at the date of departure shall be offered to the remaining Shareholders at Fair Market Value in accordance with clause 7.

### 3.4 Unvested Shares — Bad Leaver

If a Shareholder becomes a Bad Leaver, their unvested Shares at the date of departure shall be transferred to the remaining Shareholders (or to the Company for cancellation, as the remaining Shareholders elect) for nil consideration. The Bad Leaver's vested Shares remain subject to the transfer provisions in clause 8.

### 3.5 Vested Shares — All Leavers

A Departing Founder's vested Shares are not automatically transferable upon departure. They remain subject to the Right of First Refusal in clause 8 and the drag-along and tag-along provisions in clauses 9 and 10. The Company and remaining Shareholders have the right (but not the obligation) to purchase the Departing Founder's vested Shares at Fair Market Value within 90 days of the departure event.

### 5.2 Deadlock

If the Shareholders are unable to reach agreement on a Reserved Matter within 30 days of the matter being raised (a **"Deadlock"**):

(a) the parties must first attempt to resolve the Deadlock through good faith negotiation for a further 15 Business Days;

(b) if the Deadlock is not resolved, the parties must refer the matter to mediation in accordance with clause 18; and

(c) if mediation fails to resolve the Deadlock within 30 days, any Shareholder may serve a **Buy-Sell Notice** under clause 5.3.

### 5.3 Buy-Sell (Shotgun) Mechanism

Upon service of a Buy-Sell Notice, the notifying Shareholder must specify a per-Share price. The receiving Shareholders must, within 30 days, elect to either: (a) sell all of their Shares to the notifying Shareholder at the specified price; or (b) buy all of the notifying Shareholder's Shares at the same per-Share price. Failure to elect within 30 days is deemed an election to sell.

### 5.4 CEO Shareholder Individual Consent — Absolute Veto Rights

The following matters require the prior individual written consent of the CEO Shareholder, in addition to any other approval required by this Agreement. This right is personal to the CEO Shareholder, is not transferable, and survives any dilution of the CEO Shareholder's percentage shareholding below a majority:

(a) **Company sale:** any Trade Sale, merger, or acquisition of all or substantially all of the assets or shares of the Company, regardless of the percentage of Shareholders approving such transaction;

(b) **IPO:** any decision to list the Company or any holding company on a public securities exchange;

(c) **CEO removal:** any resolution to remove the CEO Shareholder from their position as Chief Executive Officer or as a director of the Company, whether by Board resolution, Shareholder resolution, or otherwise — except where the CEO Shareholder is removed for cause following a final and binding determination by a court of competent jurisdiction;

(d) **Voting dilution:** any issuance of Shares, instruments, or securities that would reduce the CEO Shareholder's voting rights below the CEO Voting Floor (clause 2.5), except where the CEO Shareholder has individually consented;

(e) **Amendment of this clause:** any amendment to this clause 5.4 or to clauses 2.5, 7.4, 7.5, or 9.1 of this Agreement; and

(f) **Change of business:** any decision to wind up, dissolve, or fundamentally redirect the Business away from the technology platform described in Recital A.

The CEO Shareholder's veto right under this clause 5.4 may not be overridden by any Shareholder resolution, Board resolution, court order (except as set out in clause 5.4(c)), or future amendment to this Agreement unless the CEO Shareholder individually consents in writing.

## 7. LEAVER PROVISIONS

### 7.1 Notification

A Founder must give the other Shareholders and the Board not less than 30 days' written notice of their intention to cease involvement in the Business.

### 7.2 Leaver Shares

Upon a Founder becoming a Departing Founder, all unvested Shares held by that Founder (the **"Leaver Shares"**) are subject to the provisions of clauses 3.3 and 3.4 respectively.

### 7.3 Valuation Dispute

If the Departing Founder disputes the Fair Market Value determined for their vested Shares, they may request an independent valuation at their own cost. If the independent valuation differs from the Company's valuation by more than 10%, the costs of both valuations shall be borne equally by the parties.

### 7.4 Key Person Protection — All Founders (Symmetric)

A **"Key Person Event"** occurs when a Founder is involuntarily removed as a director or has their active role in the Business terminated without cause and without their individual written consent.

Upon a Key Person Event affecting any Founder:

(a) all of that Founder's unvested Shares shall immediately and automatically vest in full, as if the Vesting Period had been completed (single-trigger acceleration on involuntary removal);

(b) the Company's right to require the Founder to transfer or forfeit any Shares is suspended for a period of 90 days, during which the Founder may elect to retain their Shares, sell them under the ROFR process in clause 8, or exercise tag-along rights under clause 10; and

(c) the IP assignment in clause 11.1 shall, with respect to that Founder's individual contributions, automatically convert from an outright assignment to an exclusive, royalty-free, perpetual licence granted by that Founder to the Company, with the Founder retaining underlying ownership. This licence shall remain in force for so long as the Company continues the Business and does not enter insolvency.

The intent of this clause is to ensure that no Founder can be involuntarily stripped of the economic benefit of their contribution without that Founder retaining meaningful rights and recourse.

### 7.5 CEO Shareholder — Additional Key Person Protection (Nuclear Deterrent)

In addition to the symmetric protections in clause 7.4, if a Key Person Event occurs specifically with respect to the CEO Shareholder:

(a) the Company's exclusive licence to use all Intellectual Property assigned or licensed under clause 11 (including all IP contributed by all Founders and developed by the Company) is automatically **suspended** with immediate effect, pending either:

   (i) the reinstatement of the CEO Shareholder to their role as Chief Executive Officer and director; or

   (ii) the execution of a written settlement agreement between the CEO Shareholder and the Company on terms individually agreed by the CEO Shareholder;

(b) no new products, features, or services may be developed using or derived from the suspended IP during the suspension period;

(c) the suspension does not affect existing customer contracts or orders already in progress at the time of the Key Person Event, which may continue to be fulfilled for a period not exceeding 60 days from the date of suspension; and

(d) the CEO Shareholder must act in good faith in negotiating any settlement under clause 7.5(a)(ii) and must not unreasonably withhold or delay consent to a resolution.

The Shareholders acknowledge that this clause 7.5 is a deliberate and proportionate deterrent against the wrongful removal of the CEO Shareholder, whose technical contributions are foundational to the Business, and that its inclusion is a material inducement to the CEO Shareholder entering into this Agreement.

## 8. TRANSFER RESTRICTIONS AND RIGHT OF FIRST REFUSAL

### 8.1 Restriction on Transfer

No Shareholder may Transfer any Share without the prior written consent of all other Shareholders, except as expressly permitted by this Agreement.

### 8.2 Right of First Refusal

If a Shareholder (the **"Selling Shareholder"**) wishes to Transfer any Shares to a third party, they must first give written notice to the Company and to all other Shareholders (the **"ROFR Notice"**) specifying:

(a) the number of Shares proposed to be Transferred;

(b) the identity of the proposed transferee;

(c) the proposed price per Share; and

(d) all other material terms of the proposed Transfer.

### 8.3 Exercise Period

The Company (by unanimous Board resolution excluding the Selling Shareholder) and then the remaining Shareholders (on a pro-rata basis) each have 20 Business Days from receipt of the ROFR Notice to elect to purchase all (but not less than all) of the Shares offered at the price and on the terms specified in the ROFR Notice.

### 8.4 Lapse

If the right of first refusal is not exercised within the Exercise Period, the Selling Shareholder may Transfer the Shares to the identified third party at no less than the price specified in the ROFR Notice, provided the Transfer is completed within 60 days. If not completed within 60 days, the ROFR process must recommence.

### 8.5 Permitted Transfers

A Shareholder may Transfer Shares without triggering clause 8.2 to: (a) a wholly-owned company of the Shareholder; or (b) a family trust of which the Shareholder is the sole beneficiary and trustee — provided the transferee first executes a Deed of Accession agreeing to be bound by this Agreement, and provided the transferring Shareholder remains jointly and severally liable with the transferee for all obligations under this Agreement.

## 9. DRAG-ALONG RIGHTS

### 9.1 Drag-Along Right

If Shareholders holding at least seventy-five percent (75%) of the issued Shares (the **"Dragging Shareholders"**) approve a Trade Sale to a bona fide arm's length third party purchaser, **and the prior individual written consent of the CEO Shareholder has been obtained**, the Dragging Shareholders may require all remaining Shareholders (the **"Dragged Shareholders"**) to sell their Shares on the same terms and at the same per-Share price as the Dragging Shareholders (a **"Drag-Along Sale"**), by giving not less than 20 Business Days' written notice (the **"Drag-Along Notice"**). The CEO Shareholder's consent under this clause is personal to the CEO Shareholder, is not transferable, and may be withheld in the CEO Shareholder's absolute discretion. No Drag-Along Notice shall be valid or effective unless accompanied by written evidence of the CEO Shareholder's consent.

### 9.2 Conditions

The drag-along right under clause 9.1 may only be exercised where:

(a) all Shareholders receive the same per-Share price and the same form of consideration;

(b) no Shareholder is required to give warranties or representations other than as to title and authority to sell; and

(c) each Shareholder's liability under any warranty or indemnity is capped at the proceeds received by that Shareholder.

### 9.3 Obligation to Comply

Upon receipt of a valid Drag-Along Notice, each Dragged Shareholder must execute all documents and take all steps reasonably required to give effect to the Drag-Along Sale within the timeframe specified.

## 10. TAG-ALONG RIGHTS

### 10.1 Tag-Along Right

If a Shareholder proposes to Transfer Shares that would result in the transferee holding more than 30% of the issued Shares, the remaining Shareholders have the right (but not the obligation) to require the proposed transferee to purchase their Shares on the same per-Share terms (a **"Tag-Along Right"**), by giving written notice (a **"Tag-Along Notice"**) within 15 Business Days of the ROFR Notice under clause 8.2.

### 10.2 Pro-Rata Participation

Each Shareholder exercising a Tag-Along Right may tag up to their pro-rata proportion of the total Shares proposed to be sold. If the proposed purchaser is unwilling to purchase all tagged Shares, the Transfer may not proceed without the written consent of all tagging Shareholders.
